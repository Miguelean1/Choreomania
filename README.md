**Choreomania: The Last Ascent** is a narrative web experience that flips the classic "Wheel of Death" on its head. Instead of avoiding elimination, you're fighting for selection. Sixteen participants. Four trials. One ticket to freedom aboard the *New Esperanza*.

Developed as a group project for Client Web Development, this game combines dystopian storytelling, retro-futuristic aesthetics, and a raffle system that makes hope feel like a cruel joke.

### Key Features üï∫üèΩ

* **Inverted Survival Mechanics**: A "Wheel of Hope" where being chosen means salvation, not death
* **Layered Narrative**: Cinematic intro with synchronized voice-over and unexpected turns
* **Audio-Driven Experience**: Complete sound design with narration, ambient music, and raffle effects
* **Smart Raffle Logic**: Random selection system with state persistence across rounds (16‚Üí8‚Üí4‚Üí2‚Üí1)
* **Responsive Design**: Optimized for desktop and mobile with cinematic letterboxing
* **Comprehensive Testing**: Jest-powered test suite ensuring game logic reliability

## The World of Choreomania üê∞

Year 2047. Humanoid rabbits obsessed with disco music have taken over Earth. Humans are forced to dance endlessly under their glowing eyes. Those who stop... vanish.

But once a year, the mysterious Benefactor offers an escape: the Ceremony of Ascension. One winner gets passage on the *New Esperanza* to Saturn. Or do they?

The game explores themes of control, false hope, and the price of survival in a world where freedom is just another lie.

## Development Process üôå

We followed **Agile methodology** with a hybrid approach combining solo and mob programming. Our workflow was structured around three main sprints managed through [Trello](https://trello.com/b/2VTaF0sd/choreomania-the-last-ascent):

**Sprint 1**: Conceptualization, planning, wireframing, and visual design  
**Sprint 2**: Interaction development and game logic programming  
**Sprint 3**: Testing, responsive optimization (media queries), and deployment

Regular standups, continuous integration, and iterative feedback loops kept the team aligned and the project moving forward.
